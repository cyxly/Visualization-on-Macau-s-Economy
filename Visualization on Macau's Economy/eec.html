Visitor

As Macau is a tourism-centric and consuming-driven city, number of visitors is an important indicator of its economy and worth of notice. In this part we will display and analyze visitor’s quantity from perspective: geographical distribution and entry site. More specifically, geographical distribution part reveals yearly and monthly variations of visitor numbers from different regions, while entry site part illustrates the proportion of visitors from different entry site.

Geographical distribution

As what we care here is how the number to visitors vary among different regions , the horizontal axis stands for the time (2008-1015) in each cell . And a reference line is marked as the average number of visitors of these 26 regions . The quarterly number changes are located in the right.

From which we clearly notice , only mainland China and Taiwan, Hong Kong is over this line . So to take a better look at the layout of other regions, filter is recommended.

Entry site

The series of pie chart in the left shows proportion of visitors through different entry sites since 2008, while the right part illustrates more detailed geographic distribution of visitors entering through different sites.

This is an interactive graph allowing you to easily explore data by yourself. If you want to see one specific year’s data, click that year in left part, then the right global map will display data in the year you choose correspondingly. If you want to dive deep to explore geographical distribution of visitors through one particular entry site in one particular year, you can click relevant slice in the pie chart, then the right part will jump to geographic distribution details. 

Conclusion

From this graph, we can see that the proportion distribution of visitor number from different entry site remains relative stable. Checkpoint of the Border Gate is the biggest entry site, taking up nearly half of arrivals. The second biggest entry site is Outer Harbor, while Provisional Ferry Terminal in Taipa is the third biggest entry point, followed by airport.
